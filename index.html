General Questions

What are the admission requirements?

To enroll in our programs, you must be at least 18 years old, have a valid driver's license, and pass a drug screening and physical exam. For CDL programs, you must also have a clean driving record.

Do you offer job placement assistance?

Yes, we have dedicated career services staff who work with employers to help our graduates find employment. We also host job fairs and provide resume assistance and interview preparation.

What is the class schedule like?

We offer both day and evening classes to accommodate different schedules. Day classes typically run from 8 AM to 4 PM, Monday through Friday. Evening classes are from 6 PM to 10 PM, Monday through Thursday.

Do you provide housing for students?

While we don't provide housing directly, we can assist you in finding affordable housing options near our training facilities. Many students choose to share apartments to reduce costs.

Program-Specific Questions

What is the difference between Class A and Class B CDL?

A Class A CDL allows you to operate combination vehicles with a GVWR of 26,001+ pounds, such as tractor-trailers. A Class B CDL is for single vehicles with a GVWR of 26,001+ pounds, like straight trucks and buses.

What types of heavy equipment will I learn to operate?

Our heavy equipment program covers excavators, bulldozers, backhoes, loaders, and graders. You'll receive hands-on training with each type of equipment.

Are certifications included in the program?

Yes, all necessary certifications are included in your tuition. For CDL programs, this includes preparation for your state's CDL exam. For heavy equipment, you'll receive NCCCO certification where applicable.
